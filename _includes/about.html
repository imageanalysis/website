Workflow

Below is a step-by-step guide to breast cancer analysis in Dynamika.

  • Step 1

    Upload images

    Upload your DICOM images directly through your browser or via our PACS integration. Dynamika comes with built-in regulatory compliant patient de-identification so you (and your IT staff) never have to worry about sensitive patient data leaving your browser.

  • Step 2

    Motion correction

    The patient will inevitably move a bit when acquiring multiple images for the dynamic sequence. This can obscure subsequent quantifications. Dynamika comes with built-in state-of-the-art motion correction algorithms that automatically corrects for patient movement.

  • Step 3

    Draw Regions of Interests (ROIs).

    You can draw regions of interest around the tumor using automatically generated maps from contrast-uptake curves. This information will help you discrimante healthy tissue from pathological tissue, and dynamika will automaticlyl generate statistics from your ROIs.

  • Step 4

    Generate reports

    Statistics, ROIs and images can be exported to PDF, Microsoft Word and Microsoft Excel format for subsequent statistical analysis.
